This is the note that I took during an interesting talk given by Karoline Wiesner in a colloquium in the physics department of the university of Bristol.

Truisms of Complexity

What Wiesner did here is writing down all the “very very true” statements about complexity.

  1. More is diffirent → Emergence, something new that we saw not on the individuals but only when bringing individuals together
  2. Nonliving systems can generate order
  3. Complexity can comr from simplicity
  4. Coordinated behaviours does not require an overall controller
  5. Complex systems are often modelled as networks or information processing systems
  6. There are various kinds of invariance and forms of universal behaviour in complex systems → typically, we see power law distributions
  7. Complexity science is computational and probabilistic
  8. Complexity science involves multiple disciplines
  9. There is a difference between the order that system produce and the order of the inherent system

Features of Complexity

  1. spontaneous order and self-organisation
  2. Nonlinearity → especially as a response to external signal
  3. Robustness → otherwise we can’t observe the system since it will vanish
  4. Nested structure

Conditions for Complexity

  1. Numerosity → many interactions
  2. Disorder and diversity
  3. Feedback
  4. Non-equilibrium → literially means not in thermodynamic equilibrium

Complexity in Functional Systems

  • modularity: different parts integrate different functions
  • History and Memory: system need to store informatino
  • Adaptive Behaviour: system modify its behaviour

Classical Measuring Complexity

Classical (80s and 90s) measures are all designed for “strings”

  • statistical complexity (Crutchfield, Young Grassberger, 1986)
  • Effective complexity (Gell-Mann, Lloyd, 1996)
  • Thermodynamic depth (Lloyd, Pagels, 1988)
  • Logical depth (Bennett, 1991) → How long would a computer take to generate the correlation in a string

Measuring Complexity

A newer approach, measuring different features of the complexity, there are some examples

  • Disorder: Shannon Entropy (-PlogP)
  • Feedback: Lotka-Volterra equations
  • Nonlinearity: $P(x)=cx^{-\gamma}$, looking for power law distribution
  • Order: Correlations, Mutual information (Dunleavey, 2015, Nature Comm)
  • Robustness: ACF function, fast relax → robust

We can’t compare complexity amongest different systems because the concept of complexity is multi dimensional.

My thought

It is a nice talk and good summary of science from different fields. I am a collective behaviour person and I am happy that examples of bees and ants were mentioned. I was expecting something more revolutionary and overwhelming from this talk but it did not ended up that way. IMHO, the concept of complexity is re-interpeted as a collection of multiple concepts, measured as the disorder, feedback, nonlinearity etc. This interoperation might be true (as they were deduced from truisms) but the complexity of the universe shuold be more universal in my naïve fantasy. Anyway, I will have to read more related work to give a fair comment.
